This simulation compares the rate of energy transfer from heated metal blocks to ice water in two beakers, each under its own set of initial conditions. When the green "Start" button is clicked, one or more hot metal blocks drop into beakers containing ice water, and the water temperature and/or the mass of ice melted is plotted versus time by selecting the appropriate radio button. The number of blocks and their properties are adjusted with sliders. Checking "Add stir bar" increases the heat transfer rate. Comparisons can be made by choosing different conditions for beakers 1 and 2. To change the initial conditions for another experiment, first press the Reset button. This simulation assumes perfect mixing, no temperature gradient in the metal blocks, and that all energy transferred from the blocks goes into melting the ice. Once the ice fully melts, the energy goes into raising the water temperature.
