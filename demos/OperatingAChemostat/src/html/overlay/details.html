Details

Rates of cell growth \(r_g\), cell death \(r_d\), and substrate consumption \(-r_s\) are:

\(r_g = \mu C_c\),   \(r_d = k_d C_c\),   \(-r_s = Y_{s/c} r_g\),

\(\mu = \mu_{\max} \dfrac{C_s}{k_s + C_s}\),

where \(\mu\) is the specific cell growth rate (1/h), \(C_c\) and \(C_s\) are the cell and substrate concentrations (g/L), \(k_d\) is the specific death rate constant (1/h), \(Y_{s/c}\) is the yield coefficient, \(\mu_{\max}\) is the maximum specific growth rate (1/h), and \(k_s\) is the Monod constant (g/L).

The cell and substrate concentrations are found by solving the CSTR mass balances at steady-state:

\(0 = -D C_c + r_g - r_d\),

\(0 = D(C_{s,0} - C_s) + r_s\),

solving for \(C_c\) and \(C_s\):

\(C_c = Y_{c/s} \dfrac{D(C_{s,0} - C_s)}{\mu}\),

\(C_s = -k_s \dfrac{D + k_d}{D + k_d - \mu_{\max}}\),

where \(D\) is the dilution rate (1/h), \(C_{s,0}\) is the feed substrate concentration (g/L), and \(Y_{c/s} = -1/(Y_{s/c})\).

Wash-out (\(D_{\max}\)) occurs when the cell concentration is zero:

\(D_{\max} = -\dfrac{C_{s,0} k_d + k_d k_s - C_{s,0} \mu_{\max}}{C_{s,0} + k_s}\).

The maximum rate of cell production (\(D_{\max\mathrm{prod}}\)) is where \(D C_c\) is greatest, or:

\(\dfrac{\mathrm{d}(D C_c)}{\mathrm{d}D} = 0\),

\(D_{\max\mathrm{prod}} = Y_{c/s} D \left[ \dfrac{ 2(D + k_d) k_s (D + 2k_d - 2\mu_{\max}) + C_{s,0}(D + 2k_d)^2 }{ 2(D + k_d)^2 (D + k_d - \mu_{\max}) }\right]\).

\(D_{\max}\) and \(D_{\max\mathrm{prod}}\) are in units of 1/h.
