Directions

This Demonstration uses the Peng-Robinson equation of state for mixtures to plot isotherms for n-butane(1)/n-octane(2) mixtures on a log pressure versus log volume graph. Select the temperature with a slider. Selecting the mole fraction x1 of the liquid yields the isotherm for the liquid in blue. The green isotherm is for the vapor phase (mole fraction y1) that is in equilibrium with the liquid; the values of y1 and pressure are determined from Raoult's law and are displayed in the upper-right corner. The dashed black line connects the blue isotherm to the green isotherm at the VLE pressure.

Details

The Peng-Robinson equation of state for mixtures is used to plot pressure P versus volume V on a log-log graph: $$ P = \frac{R \cdot T}{V-b_m}-\frac{a_m}{V^2+2 \cdot V \cdot b_m-b^2_m} $$ where R is the gas constant ([cm3 MPa]/[molK]), T is temperature (K), P is in MPa and V is in (cm3/mol).

am is the attraction parameter and bm is the repulsion parameter for the mixture:

$$ a_m = \sum_{i=1}^{2} \sum_{j=1}^{2} z_i z_j (1 - k_i,j) \sqrt{a_i a_j} $$

$$ b_m = \sum_{i=1}^{2} z_i b_i $$

where z is the component mole fraction, kij is the binary interaction parameter and a and b are the attraction and repulsion parameters for a pure component.

The binary interaction parameter can be calculated [1]:

\( k_{i,j} = 1 = \frac{1}{2} \frac{b_2}{b_1} \sqrt{\frac{a_1}{a_2}} - \frac{1}{2} \frac{b_1}{b_2} \sqrt{\frac{a_2}{a_1}} + \frac{1}{2} \frac{b_2 R T}{\sqrt{a_1 a_2}} \frac{\Theta_1}{\left(\frac{T}{T_{c,1}}\right)^{\Theta_2}}, \) for an alkane/alkane mixture \( \Theta_1=0.22806 \) and \( \Theta_2=0.18772. \)

The attraction and repulsion parameters for a pure component are: \[ a = 0.457 \frac{R^2 T_c^2}{P_c} \left( 1 + \kappa \left( 1 - \sqrt{\frac{T}{T_c}} \right) \right)^2 , \] \[ b = 0.0778 \frac{R T_c}{P_c} \] where Tc is the critical temperature (K) and Pc is the critical pressure (MPa). \[ \kappa = 0.37464 + 1.54226 \, \omega - 0.26992 \, \omega^2 \] where \( \kappa \) is a simplification term and \( \omega \) is the acentric factor.

Raoult's law is used to calculate the pressure of the mixture at VLE: \[ P_{\text{vle}} = x_1 P_1^{\text{sat}} + (1 - x_1) P_2^{\text{sat}} \] and Pvle is used to determine the vapor mole fraction: \[ y_1 = x_1 \frac{P_1^{\text{sat}}}{P_{\text{vle}}} \] Where \( x_1 \) and \( y_1 \) are the liquid and vapor mole fractions of hexane, and \( P_i^{\text{sat}} \) is the saturation pressure that is calculated using the Antoine equation: \[ P_i^{\text{sat}} = 10^{\left( A_i - \frac{B_i}{T + C_i} \right)} \] where Ai, Bi and Ci are Antoine constants. The screencast video at [2] explains how to use this Demonstration.
