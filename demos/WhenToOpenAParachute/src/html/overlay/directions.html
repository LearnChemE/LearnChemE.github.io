Directions

Learning objective:
(1) Understand force balance during a skydive and how drag changes after deployment.
(2) Explore how parachute size and jump altitude affect landing safety.

How to use this demo:

  • Set the jump altitude and parachute diameter using the sliders.
  • The parachute automatically opens at t = 30 s (shown by the dotted line).
  • Aim to land at <= 3.5 m/s. The green band on the speed plot highlights a typical safe range (2.5-3.5 m/s).
  • Outcome messages (exactly one will show):
    - safely landed
    - hit the ground at unsafe speed since parachute never opened
    - hit the ground at unsafe speed since parachute too small
  • If the ground is not reached within 360 s, the demo still classifies the outcome using the post-deployment terminal speed: within the safe range => "safely landed", otherwise => "too small".

Questions to consider:

  • Why does opening higher generally make a safe landing more achievable?
  • For the same altitude, how does increasing diameter shift the speed curve after t = 30 s?
  • Why is the final speed at touchdown not always equal to the post-deployment terminal speed?
