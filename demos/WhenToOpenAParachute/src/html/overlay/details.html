Details

The equation of motion is:

\[ m \frac{dv}{dt} = mg - \rho \frac{v^2}{2} \, C_{Dh} A_h \; - \; \rho \frac{v^2}{2} \, C_{Dp} A_p \, H\!\left(t - t_{\text{open}}\right). \]

where \(m\) is the mass of the person, \(g\) is gravity, \(v\) is velocity, \(C_{Dh}\) is the human drag coefficient, \(A_h\) is the human projected area, \(C_{Dp}\) is the parachute drag coefficient, \(A_p\) is the parachute projected area, and \(H\) is the Heaviside step function, defined by

\[ H(x) = \begin{cases} 0, & x < 0 \\ 1, & x \ge 0. \end{cases} \]

The Heaviside term turns on the parachute drag at \(t \ge t_{\text{open}}\). The equation of motion is integrated with \(v(0) = 0\) and

\[ -\frac{dh}{dt} = v, \]

with \(h(0) = 0\).

In this simulation, the parachute opens at a fixed time of \(t_{\text{open}} = 30\,\text{s}\). You can explore how changing the parachute diameter affects the post‑deployment (terminal) speed and the landing velocity. A safe landing speed is in the range 2.5–3.5 m/s and the green band on the velocity plot indicates this range.
