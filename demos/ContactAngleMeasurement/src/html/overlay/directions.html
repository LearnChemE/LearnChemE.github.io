Directions

Learning objective: (1) Understand the competition between the surface tension and gravity in setting droplet shape. (2) Impact of contact angle.
Qualitative understanding: Select a droplet volume from the dropdown menu and change the contact angle to observe the change in droplet shape. Try to reason why changing the contact angle changes the shape. Next, fix a contact angle and select different volumes; what differences do you observe?
Quantitative understanding: Choose a droplet volume of 10 mm3 and take three contact angles: 60°, 90° and 120°. For each position, select 6-10 points on the droplet curve. Now, conduct a least square fit on the circle using the equation (x-x0)2 + (y-y0)2 = R2 with x0, y0, and R as parameters. Plot the extracted points and the fitted curve. Do you get a good agreement? Why? Repeat the same analysis for 100 mm3.
