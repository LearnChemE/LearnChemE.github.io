Details

In this Demonstration, the height of the batch reactor is equal to the inner diameter, so reactor volume and surface area can be simplified to:

\[A = \frac{\pi}{4}h^2 + \pi h^2,\]

\[V = \frac{\pi}{4}h^3,\]

where \(A\) is the reactor surface area (cm2), \(V\) is reactor volume (cm3), and \(h\) and \(d\) are the height and diameter of the reactor and \(h = d\) (cm).

Mass and energy balances are done to get the temperature of the reactor and moles in the reactor:

\[N_{A,0} C_p \frac{dT}{dt} = \Delta H\, r_A V + U A (T_a - T),\]

\[\frac{dN_A}{dt} = r_A V,\]

\[r_A = -k_0 e^{-E_a/(RT)} C_A,\]

\[C_A = \frac{N_A}{V},\]

\[N_{A,0} = C_{A,0} V,\]

where \(N_A\) are moles of \(A\) (mol), \(C_A\) is the concentration of \(A\) (mol/cm3), the subscript 0 refers to the initial condition, \(T\) and \(T_a\) are the reaction and cooling liquid temperatures (K), \(t\) is time (min), \(r_A\) is the rate of reaction (mol/[cm3 min]), \(C_p\) is heat capacity of \(A\) (J/[mol K]), \(\Delta H\) is the heat of reaction (J/mol), \(U\) is the heat transfer coefficient (J/[cm2 K min]), \(k_0\) is the pre-exponential factor (1/min), \(E_a\) is activation energy (J/mol), and \(R\) is the ideal gas constant (J/[mol K]).
