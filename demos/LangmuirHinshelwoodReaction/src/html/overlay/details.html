Details

Assumptions:

1. The rate-determining step is the surface reaction between carbon monoxide (CO) and atomic oxygen (O).

2. Carbon dioxide (CO2) rapidly desorbs from the catalyst surface.

3. Ideal plug flow reactor

The fractional coverages \(\theta_i\) for the reaction \(\mathrm{CO} + \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{O_2} \rightarrow \mathrm{CO_2}\) are determined from Langmuir isotherms:

\[\theta_{CO} = \frac{K_{CO} P_{CO}}{\Sigma},\qquad \theta_{O} = \frac{\sqrt{K_{O_2} P_{O_2}}}{\Sigma},\qquad \theta_{vacant} = 1 - \theta_{CO} - \theta_{O},\]

\[\Sigma = 1 + K_{CO} P_{CO} + \sqrt{K_{O_2} P_{O_2}}\]

where \(K_i\) is the adsorption equilibrium constant of species \(i\) and \(P_i\) is its partial pressure.

The rate of reaction \(r\) is:

\[r = k\,\theta_{CO}\,\theta_{O} = \frac{k\,K_{CO} P_{CO}\,\sqrt{K_{O_2} P_{O_2}}}{\left(1 + K_{CO} P_{CO} + \sqrt{K_{O_2} P_{O_2}}\right)^2},\]

where \(k\) is the rate constant.

The partial pressures of CO, O2 and CO2 are calculated from material balances:

\[\frac{dP_{CO}}{dz} = -r,\qquad \frac{dP_{O_2}}{dz} = -\frac{r}{2},\qquad \frac{dP_{CO_2}}{dz} = r,\]

where \(z\) is distance down the reactor.
