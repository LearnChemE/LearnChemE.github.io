Details

The model applies Bernoulli’s equation from the inlet section to each outlet stream, assuming the outlets discharge to the ambient pressure \(p_{\text{air}}\):

\[p_1 + \frac{1}{2}\rho U_1^2 = p_{\text{air}} + \frac{1}{2}\rho U_2^2 = p_{\text{air}} + \frac{1}{2}\rho U_3^2.\]

This relates the inlet velocity \(U_1\) to the outlet velocities \(U_2\) and \(U_3\) for a specified inlet gauge pressure \(p_1 - p_{\text{air}}\). Continuity closes the system:

\[A_1 U_1 = A_2 U_2 + A_3 U_3.\]

Here \(A = \pi R^2\) is the cross-sectional area of each pipe. The simulation reports the resulting velocities and volumetric flow rates \(Q = A\,U\). The assumption of identical outlet pressures makes the two outlet velocities equal, while the individual flow rates differ because their cross-sectional areas can be different.
